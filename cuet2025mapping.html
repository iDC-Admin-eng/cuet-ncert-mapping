Economics

Class 12: Introductory Macroeconomics
Ch 2: National Income Accounting
Q.2 (Core Inflation): Based on Subsection 2.4 (Goods and Prices). The NCERT introduces price indices; core inflation is a direct extension of this.
Q.13 (GDP Calculation): Tests the core concepts of what is included/excluded from GDP, as taught in this chapter.
Q.21 (Residential Investment): An application based on the concept of investment (capital formation) from Subsection 2.1.
Q.26 (Inventory Changes): Directly relates to unintended inventory changes when desired and actual investment differ, a key concept.
Ch 3: Money and Banking
Q.4 & Q.7 (Theories of Demand for Money): Based on Subsection 3.2. While NCERT focuses on Keynes, these questions require knowledge of other historical theories on the same topic.
Q.27 & Q.54 (Monetary Aggregates): Directly tests the definitions and liquidity of M1, M2, M3, M4 from Subsection 3.3.1.
Q.48 (Statutory Liquidity Requirement - SLR): Tests the components of SLR, a key policy tool used by the central bank.
Q.49 (Keynesian Interest Rate): Based on Subsection 3.2.2, where the interest rate equates money supply and demand.
Q.63 (Liabilities of Commercial Banks): Based on the balance sheet concepts of commercial banks discussed in this chapter.
Q.66 (Money Multiplier): Tests the properties and formula of the money multiplier from Subsection 3.3.2.
Ch 4: Income Determination
Q.19 (Effect of Taxes on Multiplier): An application of the Keynesian multiplier, testing how automatic stabilizers work.
Q.32 (Balanced Budget Multiplier): A direct question on a specific case of the Keynesian multiplier.
Q.53 (Life Cycle Theory of Consumption): An advanced theory that builds upon the basic consumption function (C = f(Y)) introduced here.
Q.56 (IS Curve): The IS curve represents goods market equilibrium, derived from the core concepts of this chapter.
Ch 5: The Government: Functions and Scope
Q.15 (Government Deficits): Tests knowledge of deficit types from Subsection 5.1.3. Requires current data.
Q.64 (Public vs. Private Goods): Based on the characteristics of public goods discussed in Subsection 5.3.
Ch 6: Open Economy Macroeconomics
Q.23 (Current Account Surplus): Concept from Subsection 6.1, but requires specific current affairs knowledge.
Q.40 (Foreign Exchange Concepts): Tests practical forex terms which are applications of concepts from Subsection 6.2.
Q.69 (Balance of Payments Accounts): Tests where specific transactions are recorded, based on concepts from Subsection 6.1.
Class 12: Introductory Microeconomics
Ch 2: Theory of Consumer Behaviour
Q.3 (Utility Maximization): A direct application of the principles of consumer equilibrium from Subsection 2.3.
Q.9 (Expenditure Function): An advanced topic derived from the principles of utility maximization taught in this chapter.
Q.55 (Shifts in Demand): Tests the factors that shift the demand curve from Subsection 2.4.2 (specifically for inferior goods).
Q.71 (Consumer Surplus): The concept is from this chapter, but calculation requires integration (Maths Ch. 8).
Ch 4: The Theory of the Firm under Perfect Competition
Q.17 (Perfect Competition): Tests the conditions of equilibrium in a perfectly competitive market.
Ch 6: Non-Competitive Markets
Q.38 (Monopolistic Competition): Tests the key features of monopolistic competition from Subsection 6.2.
Q.75 (Kinked Demand Curve): A model to explain price rigidity in an oligopoly, a key concept from Subsection 6.2.
Class 11: Indian Economic Development
Ch 6: Rural Development
Q.41 (Landholding Patterns): The classification of farmers (marginal, small, medium) is part of this topic.
Ch 10: Comparative Development Experiences
Q.39 (Dependency Ratio): A demographic indicator from Subsection 10.3. The question requires specific state-wise data.

Statistics for Economics

Class 11: Statistics for Economics
Ch 2: Collection of Data
Q.62 (Sampling Methods): Based on Subsection 2.6, this question distinguishes between random (Cluster) and non-random sampling methods.
Ch 5: Measures of Central Tendency
Q.6 (Geometric Mean): A key statistical average discussed in this chapter.
Q.45 (Skewness - Mean, Median, Mode): Directly tests the relationship between these three measures to determine skewness.
Q.72 (Measures of Central Tendency): A direct matching question on the formulas for different types of means.
Ch 6: Correlation
Q.35 (Coefficient of Determination - R²): Directly related to the coefficient of correlation (r), as R² = r².
Q.51 (Correlation Analysis): Tests a key theoretical property of correlation taught in this chapter.
Q.73 (Linear Regression Model): Regression is the next step after correlation. This question tests the definition of a linear model.

Mathematics

Class 12: Mathematics (Part 1 & 2)
Ch 3: Matrices
Q.20 (Symmetric Matrices): Directly tests the properties from Subsection 3.6.
Q.31 (Matrix Multiplication): A fundamental calculation from Subsection 3.4.
Ch 7: Integrals
Q.14 (Total Cost from Marginal Cost): A classic application of integration to find a total function from a marginal function.
Ch 12: Linear Programming
Q.30 (Linear Programming): Asks for the sequence of solving an LPP graphically, as taught in Subsection 12.2.
Ch 13: Probability
Q.5 (Probability Distribution): A direct application from Subsection 13.6.
Q.10 (Expected Value): A direct calculation of the mean of a random variable from Subsection 13.6.
