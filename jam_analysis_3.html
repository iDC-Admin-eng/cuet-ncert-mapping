Primary Book: Hal Varian, Chapters 2-8, 15.

Q.21 (MCQ): Concept of Quasi-linear utility functions (U = y + f(x)).

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 4 (Utility), Section 4.4 "Quasilinear Utility".

Q.22 (MCQ): Definition of an Engel curve.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 6 (Demand), Section 6.2 "Engel Curves".

Q.23 (MCQ): Purpose of Revealed Preference theory.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 7 (Revealed Preference), introduction and Section 7.2 "The Weak Axiom of Revealed Preference".

Q.29 (MCQ): Indifference curve for a 'good' and a 'bad'.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 3 (Preferences), Section 3.4 covers "Bads", showing positively sloped indifference curves.

Q.30 (MCQ): Definition of Marginal Utility.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 4 (Utility), Section 4.2 "Marginal Utility".

Q.50 (NAT): Utility maximization with a Cobb-Douglas utility function (U=xy).

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 5 (Choice). The core concept is the tangency condition (MRS = Price Ratio), which is the main subject of this chapter.

Q.57 (NAT): Calculating price elasticity from a demand function.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 15 (Market Demand), Section 15.2 "Elasticity". This question requires applying the formula ε = (dQ/dP) * (P/Q).

Primary Book: Hal Varian, Chapters 16, 23-26.

Q.5 (MCQ): Shut-down condition for a perfectly competitive firm.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 23 (Firm Supply), Section 23.3 "The Shutdown Condition".

Q.6 (MCQ): Characteristics of a natural monopoly.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 25 (Monopoly), Section 25.7 "Natural Monopoly". Characterized by high fixed costs and declining average costs.

Q.24 (MCQ): Short-run and Long-run equilibrium in monopolistic competition.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 26 (Monopolistic Competition), Sections 26.2 and 26.3 cover short-run (profits possible) and long-run (zero profit) equilibrium.

Q.37 (MSQ): Outcomes of first-degree price discrimination.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 25 (Monopoly), Section 25.2 "First-Degree Price Discrimination". The monopolist captures the entire surplus, so Profit = Total Surplus and CS = 0.

Q.43 (NAT): Profit maximization for a multi-product monopolist.

Book Reference: Concepts are from Hal Varian, Ch. 25 (Monopoly). This requires setting up a profit function with two goods and two prices, and taking partial derivatives with respect to each price to find the maximum.

Q.47 (NAT): Profit maximization with third-degree price discrimination.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 25 (Monopoly), Section 25.4 "Third-Degree Price Discrimination". The key is to set MR=MC in each market separately.

Q.56 (NAT): Calculating the Lerner Index (measure of market power).

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 25 (Monopoly), Section 25.6 "Markup Pricing". The Lerner Index (P-MC)/P equals 1/|ε|.

Primary Book: Hal Varian, Chapters 19-22.

Q.14 (MCQ): Testing for returns to scale in a Cobb-Douglas function.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 19 (Technology), Section 19.3 "Returns to Scale". The sum of exponents in a Cobb-Douglas function determines the returns to scale.

Q.20 (MCQ): Identifying a valid cost function form.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 21 (Cost Minimization). A cost function expresses cost as C(w,r,Q). Option A is an isocost line, not a cost function.

Q.25 (MCQ): Relationship between linear AVC and the MC curve.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 22 (Cost Curves), Section 22.4 "Marginal Cost". If AVC=aQ, TVC=aQ², so MC=d(TVC)/dQ=2aQ. MC is linear with twice the slope of AVC.

Q.38 (MSQ): Conditions for diseconomies of scale (Cost elasticity, MC vs AC).

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 22 (Cost Curves), Section 22.6. Diseconomies of scale implies average cost is increasing, which occurs when MC > AC. Cost elasticity with respect to output is > 1.

Primary Book: Hal Varian, Chapters 29-30.

Q.48 (NAT): Finding the Nash Equilibrium and sum of payoffs in a simultaneous game.

Book Reference: Hal Varian, Ch. 29 (Game Theory), Section 29.2 "Nash Equilibrium". The method involves finding the best response for each player given the other's strategy.

Primary Book: Hal Varian, Chapters 36-37.

Q.39 (MSQ): Causes of market failure (Externalities, Public Goods, Market Power).

Book Reference: Varian covers these in Ch. 36 (Public Goods), Ch. 37 (Externalities), and Ch. 25 (Monopoly, as a source of inefficiency).

Q.19 (MCQ): Definition of investment in national income accounts.

Book Reference: Mankiw, Ch. 2, Section 2-1, subsection on "The Components of Expenditure". Investment is spending on new capital goods. Buying an old house is a transfer of assets.

Q.49 (NAT): Calculating the velocity of money (using Quantity Theory of Money identity).

Book Reference: Mankiw, Ch. 5 (Money and Inflation), Section 5-1 "The Quantity Theory of Money". The question uses the identity MV=PY.

Q.59 (NAT): Calculating private saving from national income accounts identity.

Book Reference: Mankiw, Ch. 3 (National Income), Section 3-1. Private Saving is defined as disposable income minus consumption, S = (Y-T)-C.
